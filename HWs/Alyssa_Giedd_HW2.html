The probability of rolling snake eyes is $\frac{1}{36}$, but the probability of rolling 7 is $\frac{6}{36}= \frac{1}{6}$. The ratio of these probabilities are 1:6.

The standard deviation of an average of several Gaussian distributions results in a much lower standard deviation than a single gaussian dstribution, the same goes for a sum of Gaussian distributions. This means that these altered forms will not display the same variance as the original signal, and interpreting the uniformity of these distributions as an indication of uniformity in the source (especially if the source is being monitored over time and there is potential for background signals to interfere in taking measurements) is bound to lead to faulty readings.
